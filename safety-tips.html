Personal Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings

    Always pay attention to your environment, especially in unfamiliar areas. Avoid distractions like using your phone while walking in public places.

  • Share your location

    Always inform trusted family members or friends about your whereabouts, especially when traveling alone or to new places.

  • Emergency contacts

    Keep emergency contacts on speed dial. Consider setting up emergency SOS features on your phone for quick access.

  • Trust your instincts

    If a situation or person makes you uncomfortable, trust your gut feeling and remove yourself from the situation immediately.

  • Secure valuables

    Avoid displaying valuable items in public areas, especially at night. Keep your belongings secure and within sight at all times.

  • Well-lit routes

    Use well-lit, populated routes when traveling after dark. Avoid shortcuts through isolated areas or alleyways.

  • Self-defense knowledge

    Consider taking self-defense classes to build confidence and learn basic techniques for emergency situations.

  • Stay connected

    Keep your phone charged and carry a portable charger when going out for extended periods.

Travel Safety Tips

  • Research your destination

    Before traveling to a new place, research about the area, local customs, safe and unsafe zones, and emergency services available there.

  • Use registered transportation

    Always use official taxis, ride-sharing services, or public transportation. Avoid unmarked or unofficial vehicles.

  • Digital and physical copies

    Keep digital copies of important documents like ID cards and tickets. Also carry physical copies as backup.

  • Share your itinerary

    Share your travel plan with trusted contacts including accommodation details and expected return time.

  • Use official prepaid counters

    At airports and railway stations, use official prepaid taxi counters instead of accepting random transportation offers.

  • Beware in tourist areas

    Be especially vigilant in crowded tourist areas where pickpocketing and scams are common.

  • Register with safety services

    For interstate travel, register with services like the Himmat Plus app or local police travelers' registration services if available.

  • Avoid late night travel

    When possible, avoid traveling alone late at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Disaster Preparedness

  • Create an emergency kit

    Prepare a kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, medications, first aid supplies, flashlight, and important documents.

  • Know evacuation routes

    Familiarize yourself with evacuation routes for your area and establish a family communication plan.

  • Flood safety

    Move to higher ground immediately; never walk or drive through flooded areas as even shallow water can be dangerous.

  • Earthquake safety

    Drop, cover, and hold on during an earthquake. Stay away from windows, exterior walls, and anything that could fall.

  • Install alert apps

    Install disaster alert apps specific to your region to receive early warnings about potential dangers.

  • Protect important documents

    Keep important documents in waterproof containers and consider digital backups in secure cloud storage.

  • Learn first aid

    Basic first aid and CPR skills can be crucial during emergencies when medical help might be delayed.

  • Written emergency contacts

    Keep emergency contact numbers written down, not just saved in your phone, in case your device is damaged or loses power.

Cyber Safety Tips

  • Strong, unique passwords

    Use complex passwords with a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use different passwords for different accounts and enable two-factor authentication when available.

  • Be cautious with personal information

    Think twice before sharing personal details online or on social media. Adjust privacy settings to limit who can see your information.

  • Verify sources

    Before clicking on links or downloading attachments, verify the source. Hover over links to see the actual URL they lead to.

  • Keep devices updated

    Regularly update your devices and applications to ensure you have the latest security patches and protection.

  • Secure online transactions

    Use secure payment methods and check for HTTPS before entering sensitive information like credit card details.

  • Public Wi-Fi caution

    Avoid accessing sensitive accounts or conducting financial transactions on public Wi-Fi networks. Consider using a VPN for additional security.

  • Report cybercrime

    Report cybercrime incidents to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and local cybercrime cells.

  • Regular backups

    Regularly backup important data to prevent loss from ransomware attacks or device failures.

Women's Safety Tips

  • Safety apps

    Install and learn to use women safety applications like Nirbhaya, Smart24x7, or government apps like Himmat Plus that offer panic button features.

  • Plan travel routes

    Plan your travel routes in advance and share your live location with trusted contacts while commuting.

  • Be alert in public transport

    In public transport, try to sit near the driver or in women-only sections if available. Be aware of your surroundings at all times.

  • Learn basic self-defense

    Learning some basic self-defense techniques can boost confidence and provide crucial skills in emergency situations.

  • Carry safety tools

    Consider carrying legal safety tools like pepper spray or personal alarms that can alert others in case of danger.

Child Safety Tips

  • Teach personal information rules

    Educate children about not sharing personal information with strangers, both online and offline.

  • Emergency contact knowledge

    Ensure children memorize essential phone numbers and home address, and know how to contact emergency services.

  • School safety protocols

    Make sure children understand school safety protocols and know who is authorized to pick them up.

  • Internet safety

    Monitor children's online activities and teach them about internet safety, including not communicating with strangers online.

  • Buddy system

    Encourage children to use the buddy system when going out, especially to public places or while commuting.
